# Triage Criteria — what makes a transcript worth deep analysis

Stage 01 (triage) is a fast classification pass over a raw transcript. Its only job is to
decide where the transcript goes next. It does not analyze the workflow in depth — it
looks for the signals that justify spending the analysis budget. Most transcripts do not
warrant deep analysis, and triage is what keeps the pipeline from drowning in them.

The bar is simple: **deep analysis is for transcripts that contain a repeated, manual,
document-heavy workflow with a named cost — and especially a decision made against written
criteria.** Everything else either gets logged as a thin opportunity or dropped.

---

## Signals worth deep analysis

Look for these. One strong signal can justify deep analysis; two or more makes it clear.

- **A repeated, manual, document-heavy workflow.** The same task, done over and over, by
  hand, over documents. "Every claim file, a reviewer reads cover to cover." "We do this
  for hundreds of appeals a month." Repetition + manual + documents is the core fingerprint
  of something Veridian can help with.
- **A named pain with a volume or time cost.** Not just "it's annoying" but a quantified or
  quantifiable burden. "Half a day per file." "A two-week appeals backlog." "Three FTEs
  just on intake." A pain with a number attached is a pain worth solving and worth demoing.
- **A decision made against written criteria.** The highest-value signal in this domain. A
  reviewer measuring a record against coverage policy, medical-necessity criteria, or a
  routing taxonomy. Written criteria mean the workflow can be encoded (Pattern 6, the
  Determination Library) — this is where the strongest demos come from.
- **Buried workflow mentions inside training or support calls.** Do not assume the gold is
  only in discovery calls. A training call where someone says "the way I actually do this
  is..." or a support call where a user describes a painful manual workaround often reveals
  a real, specific workflow that no discovery call surfaced. Read training and support
  transcripts for these asides — they are frequently the best leads.

---

## The three routing outcomes

Stage 01 produces exactly one of these three classifications for each transcript.

### 1. Deep analysis

The transcript contains a real, specific, document-heavy workflow worth profiling.
**Meaning:** route to stage 02 (workflow extraction) for a structured deep read. **When:**
one or more of the signals above is clearly present — especially a decision made against
written criteria, or a named pain with a volume/time cost. This is the path toward an
opportunity recommendation and possibly a full demo.

### 2. Log opportunity & stop

There is a plausible opportunity signal, but not enough to justify the analysis budget
right now — a passing mention, a vague pain with no volume, a workflow described too
thinly to profile. **Meaning:** record the opportunity (customer, the signal heard, why it
didn't clear the bar) and stop. Do not run stage 02. **When:** something real is there but
it is one weak signal, or the detail needed for a workflow profile simply isn't in the
transcript. Logging keeps it findable if a later transcript from the same customer adds
detail.

### 3. No action

The transcript contains no actionable workflow signal. **Meaning:** classify and stop;
nothing is logged as an opportunity. **When:** the call is purely relational, logistical,
administrative, or off-topic — scheduling, pricing-only haggling, a status check with no
workflow content. No repeated manual document workflow, no named pain, no decision against
criteria.

---

## Triage discipline

- **When in doubt between deep analysis and log-only, log-only is the safe default** — but
  if a *written-criteria decision* is present, lean toward deep analysis; it's the
  signal most likely to become a strong demo.
- **Always read training and support transcripts to the end.** The strongest workflow
  mentions are often buried mid-call, not stated up front.
- **Triage records a routing decision, not an analysis.** Keep it fast. The depth happens
  in stage 02.
