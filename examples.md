# Pitchcraft in action — Summit Ridge Health Plan

A worked end-to-end run: one raw discovery-call transcript, all six stages, a sharable preview at the end. This is the proof that the folder does what the brief asks.

## The input

Daniel Reyes, a sales engineer, gets on a 42-minute discovery call with the utilization-review team at Summit Ridge Health Plan, a regional health plan. The pain is concrete: complex prior-auth packets run 100–150 pages of unorganized provider records, a reviewer burns the first hour just orienting, and the team has written medical-necessity criteria that *should* make this a checklist — yet two qualified reviewers reach different determinations on **17% of complex cases** (one in six), almost entirely because of *what's in the file*, not the medicine. That variance feeds a **six-week appeals backlog** (~180 open cases, some on statutory clocks). All Daniel has is the talk. The transcript is the raw material: [00_input/discovery-call-transcript.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/00_input/discovery-call-transcript.md).

## Through the pipeline

- **Stage 01 — Triage.** Classifies the call as worth a deep build: a decision-against-written-criteria signal with measured variance. → [01_triage.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/01_triage.md)
- **Stage 02 — Workflow extraction.** Profiles who does what, on which documents, in what sequence, with the volumes and quoted pain signals attached. → [02_workflow_extraction.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/02_workflow_extraction.md)
- **Stage 03 — Opportunity mapping.** Maps the workflow to a lead pattern (a medical-necessity checklist Atlas Skill) and two supporting patterns, each with its honest gate, then escalates. → [03_opportunity_mapping.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/03_opportunity_mapping.md)
- **Stage 04 — Demo planning.** Turns the recommendation into a storyline: recreate the variance pain on the customer's own kind of case, then resolve it, with the gates stated out loud. → [04_demo_plan.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/04_demo_plan.md)
- **Stage 05 — Asset generation.** Builds the working materials: a consolidated claim file, a real reusable checklist Skill definition, and the saved demo prompts. → [claim-file-consolidated.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/05_assets/claim-file-consolidated.md) · [medical-necessity-checklist-SKILL.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/05_assets/medical-necessity-checklist-SKILL.md) · [prompts.md](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/05_assets/prompts.md)
- **Stage 06 — Delivery package.** Produces a self-contained one-page HTML preview the rep can send before the meeting. → [06_preview/preview.html](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/06_preview/preview.html)

## The output

The deliverable is the [rendered preview](examples/summit-ridge-health-plan/06_preview/preview.html): a clean memo showing Summit Ridge their own workflow, the proposed solution, and the honest gates — sent before anyone burns the team's time on a meeting.

This is the proof. A generic 42-minute transcript went in; what came out is a tailored, cited demo built on the customer's own kind of case — quoting their own words, naming their own document types and volumes, and carrying the honest limits of every recommendation. That is the gap between this and the forty generic slide decks the customer said she'd already seen.
